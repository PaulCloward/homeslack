How do I receive an offer on my home?
1
Verify your information
After we pull your address, verify the information is correct
View more

After entering your home address in the search box above, we will give you a chance to edit any of the information you see that is not correct. Sometimes your home does not match our records exactly, especially if you have modified your home. Examples may include bedroom count, bathroom count, finished square-footage, pool, or garage.

Once you have edited any information that may be incorrect, move on to “Select a Time-Frame."

View less
2
Select a time-frame
Tell us when you need the money
Tell us when you will be out of your home
View more

After you verify your information, it is time to select your time-frame. In this section you will outline two important details:

When you need the money from the sale of your home?

We will need a minimum of 5 business days before we can close and release the funds to your account but feel free to give yourself as much time as you need.

When you can be out of your home?

Our investors will send all the money for the purchase of your home to the title company in charge of processing the transaction. When you are out of the home, the money will be released to you.

Now that you have chosen your time frame, it's time to upload pictures of your home! These will be the pictures that investors will use to assess the condition of your property. Investors are willing to pay more for homes that have detailed pictures. It's best to include the good and the bad.

Here's a few tips to help improve the quality of your photos:
  1. Hold your camera steady
  2. Good lighting
  3. Take horizontal photos (landscape)
  4. Minimum of 20 photos

Homeface has partnered with real investors in your area who buy houses for cash. Cash means the transaction is not subject to loan qualifications such as an appraisal or inspections. Cash means you do not have to sit and wait, hoping that your potential buyer will qualify for a loan. Cash means your buyer has the money available, and is ready to close as soon as you are!

All closings are done through a qualified title and escrow company who will explain each step of the process. They will make sure that the title to your home stays in your name, until you sign the paperwork in their office or in front of a licensed notary. *You must sign the closing documents in person.

The title company will pay you the money from your home after the closing is finalized.
